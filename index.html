My Skills

Japanese

I have passed the JLPT N2. I am comfortable reading, writing, and communicating in Japanese.

Leadership

I have experience leading a team of over 15 teachers, spread accross 4 branches. I am confident in my ability to communicate important information to a diverse group of people.

Curriculum

In my current position, I plan the curriculum for two courses geared at teaching English to elementary school children. I design curriculum materials and coordinate with a number of people to deliver them to teachers.

Technology

Coming into the MLIS program with 0 technical background, I have grown comfortable using markup and coding languages such as Python, R, SQL, HTML, and CSS. One of my greatest takeaways from this program is that I love technology and data. I look forward to developing my skills in this area for the rest of my life.

Projects

Database Design

This is the Entity Relationship Diagram and design for a fictional movie review database. This database included column encryption of user passwords.

Tokyo Trains

I created a dataset of every train station in Tokyo, Japan. Using R, I created an interactive map visualizing these 600+ stations. I used this visualization to tell a story about the privatization of transportation in Japan.

  • Learn More

Data Curation

Using open access data from PsychArchives, I created a repository on GitHub with curated data of a database. This includes database files, the database metadata in XML according to DataCite standards, a readme, and a final report.

  • Learn More

A Taxonomy and Ontology of Shakespeare

I made a thesaurus on Shakespeare Studies for first year university students. Based on this thesaurus, I developed a taxonomy for a client selling Shakespeare seminars and an ontology to describe the domain of Shakespeare studies as a whole.

Foodie Fanatics

This is a collection of information architecture work done for the fictional website Foodie Fanatics. It includes a business case, user personas, a card sort, a site map, a roadmap, and wireframes.
